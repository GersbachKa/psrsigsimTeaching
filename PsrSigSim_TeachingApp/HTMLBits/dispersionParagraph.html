Dispersion

Dispersion is another effect that we must correct for when searching for new pulsars or analyzing a known pulsar's data. Dispersion is an effect where light is delayed by passing through a medium. The effect is frequency dependnt and lower frequencies from a pulsar get delayed more than higher frequencies. The amount of delay is dependent on the amount of material (free electrons, also called the interstellar medium or ISM) between the pulsar and the observer, as well as the frequencies being observed.
